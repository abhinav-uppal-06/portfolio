05
Organisational Behaviour · Change Management
Ecomm Edge Motivation & Change Diagnostic
Diagnosed the root cause of a 57% drop in closing rates at an Indian D2C accelerator following an organisational restructure. Applied Job Demands-Resources model and produced strategic recommendations to reverse disengagement.
Oct – Dec 2025
Project Overview
Ecomm Edge is India's premier D2C e-commerce accelerator, serving 10,000+ entrepreneurs. Following a structural change in November 2025 the removal of pre-qualifier sales roles the company saw a sharp and sudden decline in performance. Average closing rates dropped from 32% to 14%, show-up rates fell from 75% to 55%, and average calls per closer dropped from 80 to 45. Revenue declined in line from October onwards. This engagement diagnosed why, using structured OB frameworks and primary research.
Key Findings & Recommendations
Root cause: role expansion without resource rebalancing closers faced 3x workload increase (screening + closing) with no additional training, tools, or compensation adjustment
Applied Job Demands-Resources (JD-R) model: demands increased sharply while resources (autonomy, clarity, support) collapsed classic burnout and disengagement trigger
Employee wellbeing data: workload at 90%, confidence at 50%, autonomy at 40%, role clarity at 30%
Recommended: AI/WhatsApp chatbot pre-qualification to restore lead filtering without headcount cost; visible leadership re-engagement; "Living Playbook" for standardised procedures
Phased 5-week implementation roadmap: Tech Deployment → Leadership Re-Engagement → Zero-Friction Launch

Organisational Behaviour · Research Presentation
Perceptual Biases in Hiring Academic Research
Co-presented research on ethnic and gender bias in recruitment, drawing on a 44-year meta-analysis (Schaerer et al., 2023). Found minority applicants submit 50% more applications for equal interview rates. Facilitated class discussion from a consulting lens.
Oct – Nov 2025
Overview
This group presentation examined the mechanisms and consequences of perceptual bias in hiring decisions, grounded in peer-reviewed research. The key framing viewing bias reduction not just as an ethical imperative but as a business performance issue gave the session a consulting orientation that generated strong class discussion.
Key Research Findings Presented
Minority applicants submit ~50% more applications than equally qualified majority applicants to secure the same interview rate (Zschirnt & Ruedin, 2016)
Gender bias in hiring has declined significantly over 44 years for male-typed and gender-neutral roles, but persists in female-typed roles (Schaerer et al., 2023)
Four bias reduction mechanisms: anonymisation, standardisation, algorithmic screening, and accountability panels
Consulting angle: organisations that address bias outperform on innovation and talent retention making it a strategic, not just ethical, priority

Project Management · E-Learning Course
Corporate Burnout: Causes, Risk & Business Impact
Co-developed a 60-minute interactive training course on workplace burnout. Led the design and delivery of 'Module 2', focusing on the Job Demands-Resources (JD-R) model and quantifying the financial costs of presenteeism and turnover.
Jan – Apr 2026
Project Overview
Designed a comprehensive, interactive corporate training blueprint titled "Understanding, Preventing & Responding to Workplace Burnout". The 60-minute course integrated filmed scenario analysis with structured academic modules. Working in a team of 5, I took ownership of Module 2, bridging the gap between the psychological experience of burnout and its measurable financial impact on the organisation.
Key Contributions — Module 2
The JD-R Diagnostic: Framed the Job Demands-Resources model (Demerouti et al., 2001) as a practical diagnostic tool for managers, shifting the focus from "employee weakness" to structural resource deficits.
The Business Cost: Quantified the commercial impact of burnout, specifically highlighting that presenteeism (invisible underperformance) costs organisations significantly more than visible absenteeism.
Interactive Design: Integrated interactive LMS elements, including hotspot diagrams and scenario-based branching, which evidence shows increases knowledge retention by up to 40%.
Remote Work Risks: Incorporated specific risk factors for hybrid teams, addressing boundary blurring and the "always-on" culture.
